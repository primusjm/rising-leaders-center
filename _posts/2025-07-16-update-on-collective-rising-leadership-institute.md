Leadership was never meant to be a solo act. It’s a journey we’re meant to walk together — building, learning, and rising as one.

Across decades as a grantmaker, urban development researcher, professor, and now as a coach, I keep returning to the same question: Why do we keep focusing on individual leaders when real change in communities is collective?

In 2023, we launched the Collective Rising Leadership Institute and its first cohort of leaders not with answers, but with a set of questions to explore:

- What becomes possible when leadership development isn’t about shaping individuals to lead alone, but about nurturing teams to grow together, connect to purpose, and spark change from within?
- How can leaders create meaningful change with and for the communities they serve?
- What would lasting change mean for the communities we seek to serve?

Together with 35 education leaders from four organizations and two RELI country leadership networks across Kenya and Tanzania, we stepped into this experiment. We didn’t know what would unfold, but we trusted the impulse to explore a new way, together. These leaders came together as a cohort and engaged in big questions and explorations, together completing team coaching on leadership to better understand themselves, their organizations, and their communities.

How we designed the Collective Rising experience:

- Individual Awareness: We began with personalized assessments for leaders. These assessments gave leaders a mirror to their strengths, blind spots, and opportunities for personal growth. With support from learning partners and coaches, participants built the self-awareness, self-trust, and empathy needed to lead differently.

- Collective Awareness: Through integral team coaching, leaders shifted from "I" to "We." They began to learn how to lead with one another, anchored in shared purpose and responsibility to the communities they serve. Together, they celebrated one another’s strengths and started a journey of evolving as a team. 

- Skill Building & Cohort Connection: We introduced practical tools and practices around listening, communication, and relational leadership. An Immersion and a Celebration Summit deepened these lessons and built bonds across one another and across organizations.

What resulted:

Over the first nine-month cohort, and every cohort since, we’ve watched individuals change in incredibly meaningful ways. Teams and organizations likewise shift their cultures and practices to ways that support wellbeing internally and externally within the community. Importantly, every person who has walked through a CRLI program emerges with a new, stronger network and a strengthened desire to create change with and for others.

What we’re learning: Leadership shifts when individuals gain self-awareness, but it transforms when teams reorient around purpose, share leadership, and embed collaboration into how they work every day.

To the coaches, partners, and the 35 leaders who stepped into the unknown with us: Thank you. To all of our 85 (and counting!) alumni, you’ve inspired us and helped us to grow with you. And, of course, I’ve grown through this process, too: I’m gaining the ability to grow past my perfectionism and to trust myself as a coach more fully.

Today, we’re on our third cohort of leaders, many of whom are focused on girls’ education. We're still asking big questions, refining our coaching model, and learning from every interaction. We’re building and rebuilding our model of leadership coaching every day to ensure we’re helping every leader, team, and organization to become their best selves.

Testing something new is a humbling and gratifying experience. The process and the change are slower than you want, muddled by an intense desire for complexity and perfectionism. I’m proud of what we’ve built together and excited to keep sharing about Collective Rising Leadership Institute in the months ahead as we get ready to launch our new website, continue refining our gender empowerment and equity model, and distill complex learnings from all the data we’ve collected across these three cohorts. I’m more hopeful than ever that change is possible.

Until next time, let’s keep rising together.

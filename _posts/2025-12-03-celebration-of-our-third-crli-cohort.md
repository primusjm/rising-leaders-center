This past October, we marked an important milestone at the Rising Leaders Center: the completion of the coaching phase for our third cohort of the Collective Rising Leadership Institute (CRLI). This nine-month journey brings together education-focused nonprofits and community-based organizations to deepen self-awareness, strengthen team capacity, and build the conditions for lasting impact.

At our Celebration Summit, we solidified learning, shared victories, reconnected with peers, and set intentions for the work ahead. Our conversations ranged from bold aspirations—how we can collectively become the change we seek—to practical needs like work-life integration and the importance of rest. Through it all, we paid attention to connecting as individuals with ourselves through journaling and intention-setting, connecting with our peers through conversations, and connecting joyfully in our teams—celebrating, sharing, growing, and being supports for one another.

Cohort Three represented ten organizations across East Africa, each committed to advancing change with and for their communities. The photo we captured includes only a fraction of the leaders involved, but it reflects the determination and generosity they brought into every learning opportunity and coaching session.

With nine months of leadership coaching, skills development, and supports behind them, this stage of CRLI focuses on harvesting insights, mapping organizational and team transformation, implementing Organizational Transformation Action Plans (OTAPs), and continuing organizational growth with a new set of tools in the toolbox. Over the next year, we will continue to walk alongside each organization as they implement their OTAPs, embedding new habits, tools, and mindsets into their everyday work.

A few reflections stand out about this cohort of leaders:

1. A different kind of energy. Cohort Three was younger, newer, and stepping into this kind of leadership development for the first time. The growth was unmistakable. Emerging leaders found their voice. Teams aligned around a shared purpose. The passion and curiosity in the room signaled a generation eager to lead with intention and integrity.

2. Organizations on the rise. Many organizations that participated in Cohort Three were early in their journeys and facing real challenges towards achieving impact. That openness made space for experimentation, honest reflection, and real discovery. Their excitement and hunger to learn were tangible.

3. Learning as an operating model. One of the most powerful outcomes was seeing teams formalize learning as a core organizational practice. Learning buddies clarified their insights, strengthened accountability, and committed to supporting each other through the year ahead.

My favorite part of this cohort? Watching teams unite around a shared purpose. You could feel the shift when leaders recognized their strengths, understood their value, and saw their communities not just as beneficiaries, but as partners in change.

My hope for them is straightforward: continued learning, continued transformation, and continued alignment around purpose. Change doesn’t rest on one person. There is no universal solution. However, when leaders deepen their awareness of both their gifts and areas for growth, they expand what’s possible.

Each leader was strong on their own. Together, they rose even higher—crowned eagles rising in formation. Congratulations to Community Focus International (CFI), TENMET, FICH Foundation for Inclusive Community Help (FICH), GLAMI - Girls Livelihood and Mentorship Initiative, Zizi Afrique Foundation, Amani Girls Organization, Grassroots Nest for Innovations and Change (GRiC Africa), Kimanya-Ngeyo Foundation for Science and Education, Community Support Initiatives Tanzania (COSITA), and U-TENA Youth Organization.

HERE'S TO THE JOURNEY AHEAD.

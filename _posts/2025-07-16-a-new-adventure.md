Collective Rising seeks to (re)connect emerging leaders across East Africa with their purpose, with the communities they serve, and with each other. This initiative builds on my experiences and observations from decades of supporting nonprofit organizations around the world and, most recently, six years supporting the Regional Education Learning Initiative- RELI Africa to evolve into a leading voice on quality, inclusive education in East Africa. While I will continue supporting RELI Africa as a Senior Advisor, I hope Collective Rising will go further in exploring one key question: 

 “What might it look like to better support emerging leaders in East Africa to bring about transformational change with and for the communities they serve?” 
 
 This question feels particularly relevant considering this year’s United Nations General Assembly #UNGA78 where we marked the half-way point in the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals with the SDG Summit. It is clear we still have a lot of work to do, particularly in the realm of transforming education and realizing #SDG4. I won’t claim to have all the answers, but I do believe that if we are committed to escaping the cycle of “business as usual,” we need more civil society leaders practicing leadership that is collective and interdependent rather than individualistic and hierarchical; that is driven by compassion, equity, and justice for communities rather than unbridled growth and scale; that is grounded in learning and adaptation with communities rather than silver bullets and donor priorities. 
 
After years of engaging with more traditional models of leadership, I was recently exposed to a different methodology I believe has the potential to help us escape this cycle. In October 2021, I began the process of becoming an Integral Master Coach through Integral Coaching Canada. While I didn’t realize it when I began, this experience would add an additional dimension to what has been a lifelong investigation—spanning my careers as an urban development researcher, a professor, a philanthropy advisor, and a grantmaker.

 Becoming an Integral Master Coach was intensive, exhausting, and—as is this case with many such experiences—deeply rewarding and transformative. It required that I take a long, hard look in the mirror, reflect, appreciate how I came to be who I am, and consider what beliefs and behaviours it was time to leave behind so I could embody new ways of being and working.  I learned about the close connection between personal transformation and doing transformative work, about the power of presence and deep listening, and how to support others to embark on their own personal transformation journey using a structured, integral developmental process.  Now I am going to draw from this methodology as I continue to work with civil society leaders across Africa who, despite playing a central role in bringing about the change we all wish to see, do not always receive the in-depth, context-specific support they need.
 
 The Collective Rising Leadership Institute is piloting an approach to (re)connect leaders with their purpose, with their communities, and with each other. It aims to set individuals, leadership teams, and organizations on a developmental journey in which they reflect critically about their role as active brokers between communities—and their vision for the future—and the various actors who help make that vision a reality. In doing so, we hope to sow the seeds of a movement. In this movement, leaders are not only equipped with tactics and evidence, but they are also deeply self-aware and grounded in their self. They come together, listen and learn from communities, and collectively pursue transformative agendas that truly prioritize the wants and needs of the most marginalized. The program will work at multiple levels simultaneously—the individual, the leadership team, the organization, and the cohort. 
 
 Its three main components are: 
 
 1. Individual Awareness and Development 
 2. Collective Awareness and Development through group coaching 
 3. Cohort Building and Skill Development
 
 In October, I head home to East Africa to officially launch this new initiative with four organizations working across East Africa on quality, inclusive education, as well as leadership teams from the Regional Education Learning Initiative (RELI) Africa.  I will continue to share what we are discovering together as we go. There are no closed doors here. I have never stopped learning, and I know that I will continue to do so.
